# Platform Guidance

LabMentor is for authorized lab and training environments. Always follow the platform rules for the environment you are using.

## OffSec and Proving Grounds

Use LabMentor for labs, practice boxes, and personal note-taking. Do not use it during certification exams where AI/LLM assistance is prohibited by the exam rules.

Recommended use:

- parse your scan output
- organize methodology notes
- understand why a step matters
- compare your notes against a walkthrough after getting stuck

Avoid:

- exam assistance
- flag-focused automation
- walkthrough copy/paste without learning

## TryHackMe

Use LabMentor to understand rooms, organize notes, and learn from guided material.

Recommended use:

- summarize service enumeration
- explain commands
- track leads
- convert walkthroughs into methodology lessons

Avoid:

- direct answer dumping
- auto-submitting task answers

## Hack The Box

Use LabMentor to learn methodology on machines, challenges, Academy-style labs, and retired content.

Recommended use:

- structure enumeration
- track attack paths
- compare your attempt against public retired-machine walkthroughs

Avoid:

- using public walkthroughs for active machines if platform rules prohibit it
- auto-submitting flags

## Private labs

LabMentor works well for local vulnerable VMs and private ranges because it keeps notes local and focuses on methodology.
